# Business Insights

## Overview

This report summarizes the major findings from the Global Superstore Sales Dashboard (2011–2014). The analysis focuses on sales performance across time, markets, regions, customer segments, product categories, and individual products to support strategic business decision-making.

---

# 1. Annual Sales Performance

### Business Question
How has the business performed over the four-year period?

### Key Findings

| Year | Total Sales |
|------|------------:|
|2011|$2,259,511|
|2012|$2,677,493|
|2013|$3,405,860|
|2014|$4,300,041|

Sales increased every single year.

Between 2011 and 2014, annual revenue grew from **$2.26 million** to **$4.30 million**, representing an overall increase of approximately **90.3%**.

### Business Insight

The company demonstrates consistent year-over-year growth, suggesting successful market expansion and increasing customer demand.

# 8. Profit vs Discount Analysis

The scatter plot demonstrates a noticeable negative relationship between discounts and profitability.

As discount percentages increase beyond approximately **40–50%**, a larger number of transactions generate negative profit.

### Business Insight

Heavy discounting increases sales volume but substantially reduces profitability. The company should review its pricing strategy to avoid excessive discounting.

---

# Overall Conclusion

The Global Superstore business experienced strong and consistent growth between 2011 and 2014, achieving total sales of **$12.64 million** and total profit of **$1.47 million**.

The analysis indicates that:

- Revenue continues to increase annually.
- Sales peak during the final quarter of each year.
- APAC and the Central region are the strongest-performing geographical markets.
- Technology is the highest-performing product category.
- Consumer customers generate more than half of all revenue.
- High discount levels negatively affect profitability.

These findings provide clear opportunities for strategic investment, improved pricing decisions, and targeted marketing initiatives.
