# Executive Summary

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Global Superstore dataset (2011–2014) using Google Sheets to identify sales trends, profitability drivers, customer purchasing behavior, and regional performance. The objective was to transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights through data cleaning, pivot table analysis, and an interactive executive dashboard.

---

## Dataset Summary

- **Analysis Period:** 2011–2014
- **Total Sales:** **$12,642,905**
- **Total Profit:** **$1,467,457**
- **Total Quantity Sold:** **178,312**
- **Total Orders:** **25,035**
- **Unique Customers:** **4,873**

---

## Key Findings

The analysis revealed consistent business growth over the four-year period, with annual sales increasing from **$2.26 million** in 2011 to **$4.30 million** in 2014.

Sales performance was strongest during **November** and **December**, indicating a clear seasonal demand pattern. The **APAC** market generated the highest revenue among all markets, while the **Central** region recorded the highest regional sales.

Among product categories, **Technology** outperformed Furniture and Office Supplies in both revenue and profitability. The **Consumer** segment contributed over half of total company sales, making it the organization's largest customer group.

Analysis of discounts also showed that higher discount percentages are frequently associated with lower profitability, suggesting that aggressive discounting negatively impacts profit margins.
